4. Course General Description:

This course will introduce students to core methods and principles in user research, focusing on how to understand and analyze user needs, behaviors, and experiences. Students will learn how to design and conduct user research studies, including interviews, usability testing, surveys, and ethnographic field studies. The course will also cover data analysis, synthesis, and the application of research insights to the design process.

C. Course Content

  1. Introduction to User Research (3 Hours)
    • The relationship between user research, UX design, and product development
    • Introduction to the Research Lifecycle: Planning, data collection, analysis, and reporting

  2. Research Planning and Goals (4 Hours)
    • Defining research goals and objectives
    • Identifying research questions and hypotheses
    • Selecting appropriate research methods based on project goals
    • Building a research plan (timeline, resources, budget)

  3. Conducting User Interviews (8 Hours)
    • Principles of conducting user interviews: open vs. closed questions, avoiding bias
    • Techniques for building rapport and establishing trust with participants
    • Scripting and structuring interviews
    • Analyzing and synthesizing interview data

  4. Ethnographic Research and Field Studies (6 Hours)
    • Introduction to ethnographic research
    • Observational techniques and fieldwork methods
    • Taking field notes and analyzing observational data
    • Analyzing context, environment, and user behavior

  5. Usability Testing and Heuristic Evaluation (2 Hours)
    • Planning, moderating, and analyzing usability tests
    • Creating usability tasks and scenarios
    • Identifying usability problems using Nielsen’s heuristics
    • Observing and recording user interactions

  6. Survey Design and Questionnaires (6 Hours)
    • Designing effective surveys and questionnaires
    • Writing clear, unbiased questions
    • Distributing surveys and collecting responses
    • Data analysis methods for survey results

  7. Analyzing Qualitative Data (6 Hours)
    • Coding qualitative data
    • Identifying patterns and themes
    • Using tools like NVivo, Dedoose
    • Creating affinity diagrams and personas

  8. Analyzing Quantitative Data (6 Hours)
    • Descriptive statistics (mean, median, standard deviation)
    • Using visualization tools (bar charts, pie charts, histograms)

  9. Synthesizing Research Findings (10 Hours)
    • Combining qualitative and quantitative data into actionable insights
    • Creating personas and journey maps
    • Prioritizing user needs
    • Making design recommendations

  10. Communicating Research Findings (5 Hours)
    • Presenting research findings to stakeholders
    • Creating effective research reports
    • Storytelling in user research
    • Visualizing findings for non-technical audiences

  11. User Research in Iterative Design (4 Hours)
    • Incorporating research into prototyping
    • Role of research in design iteration
    • Integrating feedback into design sprints
